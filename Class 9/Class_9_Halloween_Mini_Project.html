Q11. On average is chocolate candy higher or lower ranked than fruit candy?

# average the winpercent of chocolate
# average the winpercent of fruity
# compare

#create function to find average
find_average <- function(candy_type_column_name){
  #store all winpercents of candies that have candy type
   type_winpercents <- candy$winpercent[as.logical(candy[[candy_type_column_name]])]
   
   #find the average
   average_winpercent <- mean(type_winpercents, na.rm = TRUE)
   
   return(average_winpercent)
}

Q24. What original variables are picked up strongly by PC1 in the positive direction? Do these make sense to you?

Ans: The variables that are strongly picked up in the positive direction are fruity, hard, and pluribus. These variables logically group together to define the “non-chocolate” profile, characterizing candies that are typically fruit-flavored, hard-textured, and sold in multi-piece “pluribus” packaging. PC1 therefore separates bagged fruity candies from chocolate bar–type products based on flavor and texture.
